01 · The Opportunity

Executive summary.

Maryvale has reached an inflection point. After nearly two decades of steady growth, the board has rightly identified the need to secure a permanent home: through a $5 million capital campaign. It is a significant and exciting step. It also carries significant risk if the fundraising infrastructure underneath it isn’t ready to carry the weight.

A development audit is the essential first step. Before re-launching the $5M capital campaign, Maryvale will gain a comprehensive understanding of the infrastructure needed to carry it.

Before launching any campaign, an organization needs a clear, honest picture of its current fundraising capacity: what is working, what is missing, and what must be built before major donor solicitation begins. This proposal lays out the scope, methodology, timeline, deliverables, and investment for a comprehensive development audit and strategic plan prepared specifically for Maryvale.

The audit will assess Maryvale’s fundraising culture, board engagement, donor data, communications, stewardship practices, and campaign readiness. The result is a prioritized roadmap that positions the school to pursue its campaign with confidence, credibility, and a realistic understanding of what success will require.

02 · The Picture

Where Maryvale
stands today.

Maryvale Academy of Ottawa is an independent Catholic school serving students from Junior Kindergarten through Grade 8. Founded approximately twenty years ago, the school has grown steadily and now employs thirteen full-time staff. Maryvale offers a classically-based curriculum with a strong French program and a Sacred Music Choir, distinguished by its commitment to daily adoration, weekly Mass, and authentically Catholic formation.

Despite this growth, Maryvale has never owned a building. In its nineteen years of operation, it has reported $0 in land and buildings on its CRA filings, paying an estimated $2 million in cumulative rent with no equity to show for it. The board has identified a $5 million capital campaign as the path to a permanent facility and financial stability.

03 · The Approach

Scope of audit.

The development audit is a comprehensive assessment of Maryvale’s fundraising capacity, organizational culture, and campaign readiness. It is not a compliance exercise. It is a diagnostic of the beliefs, systems, and relationships that drive, or inhibit, philanthropic investment.

Seven core audit areas

01
Organizational Culture
How the school thinks and talks about fundraising; whether development is seen as a shared responsibility or an uncomfortable necessity.
02
Board Engagement
Board members’ understanding of their fundraising role, personal giving, willingness to open doors, and capacity for campaign leadership.
03
Donor Data & Systems
Quality and completeness of donor records; CRM usage; segmentation; ability to track giving history, lapsed donors, and prospects.
04
Communications & Storytelling
Appeal letters, newsletters, impact reports, case for support, website, social media; whether the school is telling a compelling, consistent story.
05
Fundraising Programs
Current revenue streams, annual fund structure, events, major gifts, grants, planned giving; diversification and sustainability.
06
Donor Stewardship
Acknowledgment practices, recognition, reporting, relationship management; whether donors feel valued and informed.
07
Campaign Readiness
Feasibility of the $5M capital campaign; donor base depth, leadership gift potential, case strength, board capacity, organizational discipline.
Particular emphasis

Campaign readiness gets special weight. The audit will identify the specific gaps that must be addressed before a quiet phase can begin.

04 · The Strategic Plan

Strategic plan —
day one, ready to go.

The audit is paired with a written 12-month strategic development plan: a comprehensive, transparent calendar of every fundraising activity, every donor communication, and every cultivation touchpoint for the year ahead. It is a plan for consideration and discussion, not a final decree. But it means that if Maryvale brings me on as a fractional fundraiser, we hit the ground running with a plan on day one.

What the plan will include

01
Every ask
A month-by-month schedule of every solicitation: annual fund appeals (direct mail and email), year-end giving campaigns, spring major gift asks, event-based fundraising, and grant applications. Each with a target, an audience, and a channel.
02
Every communication
Newsletters, impact reports, donor emails, social media touchpoints. These are not asks, but they keep donors connected to the mission, and many will include a reply device. The plan maps every non-ask communication alongside the solicitation calendar so the board can see the full picture.
03
Stewardship rhythm
Acknowledgment timelines, recognition protocols, reporting cadence. How donors will be thanked, informed, and retained, mapped to the calendar so nothing falls through the cracks.
04
Revenue targets
Projected revenue by source and by month, grounded in the audit findings: what Maryvale can realistically expect to raise, and from where. Not aspirational guesses, but targets informed by data, donor capacity, and organizational readiness.
05
Board & staff roles
Who does what, when. Clear assignments so that fundraising is a shared organizational responsibility with defined expectations, not an ambiguous obligation that falls to whoever volunteers.
Why this matters

Most nonprofits operate without a written fundraising plan. Activities happen ad hoc, communications are reactive, and the board has no visibility into what is coming. This plan puts everything on one calendar, shared across staff and board, so that every stakeholder can see the full year at a glance, ask questions, and offer input before execution begins.

04 · The Process

Methodology.
Four phases, six to eight weeks.

The audit follows a four-phase process designed to move from data collection through analysis to actionable recommendations. Total engagement timeline: 6 to 8 weeks from receipt of initial documents.

PhaseI

Document & Data Collection

Weeks 1–2
Gather financial statements, donor data exports, communications samples, board rosters, and governance documents. Issue stakeholder interview invitations. Begin preliminary analysis of T3010 filings and CRA data.
PhaseII

On-Site Discovery

Weeks 2–3 · Ottawa
Two-day visit to Ottawa for stakeholder interviews, campus tour, and board working session. This phase generates the qualitative insights that give the quantitative data its meaning.
PhaseIII

Analysis & Benchmarking

Weeks 3–5
Analyze donor retention, giving patterns, revenue diversification, cost-per-dollar-raised, and peer benchmarks. Synthesize interview findings with data analysis. Identify patterns, gaps, and opportunities.
PhaseIV

Report & Presentation

Weeks 6–8
Draft written report and strategic plan. Review with primary contact for factual accuracy. Deliver final report and present findings to the board in person or via video.

05 · The Visit

On-site visit
& deliverables.

A two-day on-site visit to Ottawa is included. The visit serves three purposes that cannot be replicated remotely. Donors don’t give because of spreadsheets; and audits don’t work that way either.

i · Interviews

Stakeholder Interviews

One-on-one conversations with board members, the executive director, key staff, and (where appropriate) select donors or volunteers. The most diagnostic element of the audit: how people talk about fundraising reveals far more than any spreadsheet.

ii · Campus

Campus Visit

Seeing the current facility, understanding the daily experience of families and staff, and assessing what the school communicates through its physical environment. Essential context for evaluating the capital campaign case.

iii · Board

Board Working Session

A 60- to 90-minute session with the full board to present initial observations, test assumptions, and begin aligning around priorities. Not the final presentation, a collaborative diagnostic conversation.

What Maryvale receives.

Upon completion of the audit

Deliverable i

Written Audit Report

A comprehensive document presenting findings across all seven audit areas, with specific observations, benchmarks, and prioritized recommendations. Not a generic template; every finding will be specific to Maryvale.

Deliverable ii

Strategic Development Plan

A 12 month implementation roadmap with clear milestones, responsibilities, and timelines.

Deliverable iii

Board Presentation

An online presentation of findings and recommendations to the full board. Designed to build alignment and commitment, not just convey information.

Deliverable iv

30-Day Follow-Up

One follow-up call thirty days after the board presentation to answer questions, troubleshoot early implementation, and provide additional guidance as needed.
