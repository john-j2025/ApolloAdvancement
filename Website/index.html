The Problem

You're working harder than you have to. And you're not getting the results you deserve.

Bake sales. Car washes. Gala dinners that eat six months of volunteer energy and barely break even. Grant applications that take forty hours and fund ten percent of a program. You're running the playbook everyone told you to run, and wondering why the results never match the effort.

Here's why: the playbook is wrong.

The Truth

Fundraising isn't supposed to be a grind.

So many non-profit leaders feel as if they're begging their supporters and foundations to give, and as if they never receive enough. That way lies burnout and demoralization.

Here's the secret: effective fundraising isn't about begging. At its heart, it's about forging deep relationships with people who care passionately about your mission, and then offering them the opportunity to join your mission, as a partner.

Done this way, fundraising is exhilarating, fulfilling, and, yes, can even be fun.

The Difference

Every organization has a story. Most never learn to tell it.

Donors don't give because an AI-powered CRM algorithm identified them as a promising prospect. They give because a real human being sat down with them and shared a story: a story of lives transformed, communities strengthened, wonder sparked, or beauty created and shared; and then (and this is the important part), invited the donor to participate in that story, as a partner.

I'm a story-teller before I'm a consultant. My work begins with listening and finding the stories that connect your supporters with what you do, at the deepest level.

Fundraising built on relationships. Communications built on craft. Strategy built on the conviction that human beings still matter more than data points.
