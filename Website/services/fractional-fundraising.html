How It Works

Here's what it looks like to work together.

Every engagement is different, but here's the shape of a typical retainer:

  • Month 1 I learn everything. I review your donor data, your communications, your systems, and your history. I meet with your team and board. I listen more than I talk. By the end of the month, I know your organization inside out.
  • Month 2 I build the foundation. The development plan takes shape. I write the case for support, set up or clean up your donor database, design the communications calendar, and begin identifying your strongest cultivation and solicitation opportunities.
  • Months 3–6 The real work. Donor meetings, campaign launches, appeal letters, newsletters, board coaching, event strategy. I'm embedded with your team as a working partner. You're not waiting for deliverables. You're watching the program take shape in real time.
  • Months 6–12 Momentum builds. Your donor base is growing. Your board is engaged. Your communications have a voice. The systems are in place and your team knows how to use them. I'm still here, still building, but the program is becoming yours.
  • When I leave You're stronger than when I arrived. That's the measure. Not how long I stay, but what you can sustain when I'm gone.

Is This Right For You?

Is this the right fit?

Fractional fundraising isn't for everyone. That's not a sales tactic. Some organizations need a different kind of help. Here's how to tell.

This is probably right for you if:

  • You're an arts, education, or community nonprofit with a budget between $500K and $3M
  • You have a strong mission but no dedicated fundraising leadership
  • You're ready to invest in building a real development program, not just running a campaign
  • You want a partner who does the work with you, not someone who tells you what to do and leaves
  • You value relationships and storytelling over transactional fundraising tactics
  • You want your team to be stronger when the engagement ends

This probably isn't the right fit if:

  • You need a one-time strategic plan, not an ongoing partner. Consider a Fundraising Audit instead.
  • You have a specific campaign that needs management but not a full program build. Consider Campaign Development.
  • You need someone to train your board for a single session. Consider a Board Training Workshop.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

What is fractional fundraising services for nonprofits?

Fractional fundraising services means hiring an experienced fundraising professional on a retainer basis rather than as a full-time employee. The consultant embeds with your team, manages donor relationships, oversees campaigns, and builds your fundraising program - providing senior-level expertise at a fraction of the cost of a full-time Director of Development.

What size of nonprofit benefits most from a fractional development director?

Fractional fundraising services works best for nonprofits with annual revenues between $500K and $3M - organizations that have outgrown a volunteer-driven model but aren't ready to hire a full-time development director. It's especially valuable when the executive director is carrying too much of the fundraising themselves.

How is fractional development different from a fundraising consultant?

A traditional consultant delivers a project - a plan, a feasibility study - and moves on. Fractional fundraising services is ongoing, embedded partnership. I attend your meetings, join donor calls, write your appeals, and manage your fundraising program month after month, functioning as part of your team.

How long does a fractional development engagement typically last?

Most engagements run six to twelve months. The goal is always to leave you stronger - with systems, relationships, and a team that can carry the program forward. Some clients continue on a longer-term basis after the initial engagement.
