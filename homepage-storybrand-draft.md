# Homepage rewrite (StoryBrand) — draft for review

**Architecture before/after**

Current: Hero → Trust → The Vision → The Approach → The Difference → Services → About → Testimonials → Closing

Proposed: Hero → Trust → **The Problem** (was Vision) → **The Guide** (was Approach; absorbs the storytelling philosophy from the old Difference section) → **The Plan** (new; replaces Difference) → Services → About → Testimonials → Closing

The Plan section is the one structural addition. StoryBrand says a website without a visible plan is a website that doesn't convert: the visitor needs to see exactly what happens after they click, in three or fewer steps, before they will click. Your current homepage doesn't have that. Adding it is the single highest-leverage change.

The CTA is unified to one ask: **"Book a 30-Minute Strategy Call"**. Repeated 5x down the page. Same destination as your current /contact link until you wire up Calendly.

**Why the change.** The current headline is a brand line, not a buyer-frame. StoryBrand says the headline is a promise to the reader, not an introduction to the brand. "You don't need another binder. It also ladders directly to your existing line "A partner. Not a binder. Not a bot.", which is one of the strongest sentences on your current page; we are promoting it from section 2 to the hero. "Fundraising for humans" stays as a small italic tagline so the brand mark is preserved.

The subhead does the dual-buyer work: it names the ED and the board chair explicitly. That is unusual and good. It signals that you understand the actual decision unit.

## 5. THE PLAN (NEW, replaces "The Difference")

### Proposed
> Label: **The Plan**
>
> **Three steps to a fundraising program that finally works.**
>
> You have been told fundraising is complicated. It isn't. There are good development programs and improvised ones, and the path from one to the other is something you should be able to see end-to-end before you commit to anything.
>
> **01. Talk it through.**
> Book a free 30-minute call. We will talk about where you are, where you want to be, and whether what I do is the right fit for your organization. No pitch, no pressure.
>
> **02. Get the diagnosis.**
> I conduct a fundraising audit, looking at your donor base, communications, systems, board, and untapped potential, and deliver a written strategic plan you could execute on your own.
>
> **03. Build it together.**
> If you want me to lead the work, I embed with your team as your fractional development director. Writing, building, running, training, until your program is strong enough to stand on its own.
>
> [Book a 30-Minute Strategy Call]

**Why the change.** This section does not currently exist on your homepage and adding it is the single most important conversion change. Visitors who do not know what they are signing up for do not sign up. The 3-step plan removes that friction by showing the engagement is gradual: a free call, then an audit (low commitment), then optionally the fractional retainer (high commitment). It also makes step 2 a real product the visitor can buy without committing to step 3, which is good for both your business and theirs.

I will add a small piece of CSS to render the three steps with the same numbered-card visual language as your existing services grid, so the section feels native to the page.
