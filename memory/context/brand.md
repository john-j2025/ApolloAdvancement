# Apollo Advancement Brand Identity

## Visual Identity
| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Primary background | Dark near-black (#1A1210) |
| Accent color | Burnt orange (#C8461C) |
| Title font | Playfair Display serif |
| Body font | DM Sans |
| Substitute title font | Lora (when Playfair unavailable, e.g., PDF generation) |
| Substitute body font | Poppins (when DM Sans unavailable) |
| Tagline | "Fundraising for humans." |

## Report Template
A general-purpose branded Word document template exists with: branded cover page, table of contents, headers/footers, callout boxes, data tables, and closing brand block. Uses Georgia for headings, Calibri for body, burnt orange accent throughout.

## Canva
Three brand kits exist: Apollo Advancement (unnamed), Bussey Ainsworth, and Peterborough Singers. Canva was explored for blog header generation but the workflow shifted to HTML/CSS templates rendered via Playwright for more precise control.

## Voice and Tone
- Intellectually serious but warm
- Direct and practitioner-credible
- Not academic or jargon-heavy, but not dumbed down either
- Confident without being arrogant
- Empathetic toward the nonprofit leaders struggling with fundraising
- Challenges conventional thinking without being contrarian for its own sake

## Writing Rules
- No em dashes (use commas, semicolons, colons, parentheses, or separate sentences)
- No lazy transitional phrases ("Think about that for a moment," "Let that sink in")
- Conclusions emphasize organizational transformation, not just individual behavior change
- No sycophancy; push back when a prompt is incomplete or off-base
- No emojis unless explicitly requested

## Core Positioning
Relationship-focused fundraising that no AI can replace: human connection, storytelling, and belief transformation as the engine of donor growth.

## Key Messages
1. Fundraising results follow from organizational beliefs, not tactics
2. The most common dysfunction is embarrassment about fundraising
3. Fundraising is invitation, not transaction (Nouwen)
4. Donors deserve acknowledgment, specificity, and impact reporting (Burk)
5. Underinvesting in fundraising is not fiscal responsibility (Pallotta)
6. Stop apologizing for asking (Dichter)
7. Every board member has a role in development (Pitman)
