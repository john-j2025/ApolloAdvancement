## Internal Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| The nine beliefs | Blog series: "Everything You Believe About Fundraising is Wrong" (9 false beliefs nonprofits hold) |
| Fractional development | AA's primary service: embedded on monthly retainer as a client's senior fundraising partner |
| Belief transformation | John's core methodology: fundraising results follow from shifting organizational beliefs, not implementing tactics |
| The original sin | Belief #1 ("Fundraising is embarrassing"), from which most other false beliefs flow |
| The overhead myth | Dan Pallotta's concept: nonprofits penalized for investing in growth infrastructure |
| Donor-centered | Penelope Burk's approach: prioritizing donor experience, acknowledgment, and impact reporting |
| Fundraising as invitation | Henri Nouwen's concept: asking for money as inviting donors into mission participation |
| The playbook | Development Audit Playbook (internal consulting methodology) |
| The reference manual | Expanded version of the playbook with templates and appendices |
| Document 2 | Client-facing self-assessment tool (lead magnet, to be derived from the playbook) |
| Wolf Scholars | Potential full-time development role John considered; AA is the primary plan |
| First Freedoms | Nonprofit foundation associated with Bussey Ainsworth; part of marketing engagement |
