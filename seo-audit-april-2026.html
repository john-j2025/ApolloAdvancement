Executive Summary

Bottom line: apolloadvancement.com has a solid technical foundation, well-crafted meta tags, structured data on every page, and genuinely excellent blog content. But the site is functionally invisible to search engines right now. Over the past 3 months, the entire site has received only 9 clicks and 60 impressions from Google. That is not a technical problem. It is a volume, authority, and keyword-targeting problem.

Biggest strength: The blog series ("Everything You Believe About Fundraising is Wrong") is high-quality, opinionated, long-form content with strong potential to rank for fundraising-related queries. The site's technical SEO (structured data, canonical tags, meta descriptions, sitemap) is unusually clean for a solo practitioner's site.

Top 3 priorities: (1) Fix the 8 redirect errors and 1 "crawled but not indexed" page that are wasting your crawl budget. (2) Rewrite title tags and H1s to target actual search queries people type, not brand-first phrasing. (3) Dramatically increase the volume of indexed content by publishing more blog posts targeting specific long-tail keywords your ideal clients are searching for.

Internal Linking

Internal linking is weak. The blog posts link to each other within the series (good), but there are almost no links from blog content to service pages. The service pages do not link to blog posts. The homepage links to top-level pages but not to individual blog posts or service subpages. This creates shallow link equity distribution and makes it harder for Google to discover and prioritize your most important pages.

Prioritized Action Plan

Quick Wins (this week)

1. Fix redirect errors in Google Search Console

Open GSC > Pages > Redirect error. Identify the 8 specific URLs failing. Cross-reference with your _redirects file. Most likely cause: old URLs that are not covered by an existing redirect rule, or redirect chains (old URL > redirect > another redirect). Fix by adding missing rules or simplifying chains.

Impact: High Effort: 1-2 hours
2. Force www-to-non-www redirect

Google is seeing www.apolloadvancement.com as a separate site. Add a Cloudflare Page Rule or Redirect Rule that 301-redirects all www traffic to the non-www canonical. Then verify the non-www property is primary in GSC.

Impact: Medium Effort: 15 minutes
3. Rewrite title tags on core pages

Replace generic titles with keyword-targeted ones. Suggested rewrites:

4. Add lastmod dates to all sitemap entries

Several pages in sitemap.xml are missing <lastmod> tags. Add them to signal freshness to Google.

Impact: Low Effort: 15 minutes
5. Add width and height attributes to blog header images

Blog header <img> tags are missing explicit width/height, which can cause Cumulative Layout Shift. Add dimensions to prevent layout jumps during page load.

Impact: Low Effort: 15 minutes
6. Create a Google Business Profile

Set up a GBP listing for "Apollo Advancement" in Kawartha Lakes, Ontario with your service categories (fundraising consultant, nonprofit consultant). This is free and enables local pack visibility.

Impact: High Effort: 30 minutes

Strategic Investments (this quarter)

7. Publish "how-to" blog posts targeting informational keywords

Your current blog series is philosophically rich but does not target the queries people actually type into Google. Complement the fallacy series with practical posts like "How to Write a Year-End Fundraising Appeal," "How to Build an Annual Fund From Scratch," "Why Donors Stop Giving (And How to Win Them Back)." Aim for 1,500+ words, include a clear keyword in the H1 and first 100 words, and link to your service pages.

Impact: High Effort: 2-4 hours per post Goal: 2 posts/month
8. Build internal linking architecture

Every blog post should link to at least 1-2 service pages. Every service page should link to relevant blog posts. The homepage should link directly to your 2-3 highest-priority pages (fractional fundraising, blog series). This distributes link equity and tells Google which pages matter most.

Impact: High Effort: 2-3 hours (one-time)
9. Create standalone case study pages

Break out Wayside Academy and Peterborough Singers into dedicated case study pages with detailed narratives, specific metrics, and photos. These rank for organization-specific and approach-specific queries, and they build trust with prospects who find you through search.

Impact: High Effort: Half day each
10. Add a location-specific landing page

Create a page targeting "fundraising consultant Ontario" or "nonprofit fundraising help Kawartha Lakes / Peterborough." Include local context, case studies from the region, and your physical location signals. Consider a second page for US-based clients.

Impact: Medium Effort: Half day
11. Add images and more depth to service pages

Your 6 service subpages have zero images and relatively thin content (7-14 paragraphs). Add relevant imagery, expand each page to 800-1,200 words, include FAQs, and add testimonial quotes. This improves engagement signals and gives Google more content to index.

Impact: Medium Effort: 1-2 hours per page
12. Pursue backlinks through guest content and directories

The site currently has near-zero backlink authority. Target: AFP chapter websites, Imagine Canada's directory, Charity Village, nonprofit-focused publications (Nonprofit Quarterly, Canadian Fundraiser). Your blog content is strong enough to pitch for guest posts or link features. Even 5-10 quality backlinks would significantly move the needle for a site this new.

Impact: High Effort: Ongoing (1-2 hours/week)
13. Submit to fundraising consultant directories

Get listed on Clutch.co, Laridae's nonprofit consultant directory, Consultants4Good, and AFP's consultant finder. These provide both referral traffic and backlinks.

Impact: Medium Effort: 2-3 hours (one-time)

The honest assessment

Your site is 2 months old with 12 indexed pages and zero backlinks. For a brand new site, this is normal. SEO for a consulting practice like yours is a 6-12 month game, not a 6-week sprint. The good news is that your content quality is notably high, your technical foundation is clean, and you are competing in a niche (Canadian fundraising consulting) where many competitors have mediocre content. The biggest lever you can pull right now is publishing volume: more keyword-targeted blog posts, more pages, more reasons for Google to send traffic your way.

The "fallacy" series is excellent brand content, but it is not search-optimized content. You need both. The series builds trust once someone finds you. Practical how-to posts are what will get them to find you in the first place.
