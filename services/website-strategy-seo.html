What It Is

Your website should do more than exist.

Most nonprofit websites were built to check a box. They have the right pages, the right stock photos, the right footer links - and they convert almost no one. Prospective donors land on them and can't find a reason to care. Partners visit and leave with no clear sense of what the organization actually does or why it matters.

I work with organizations to fix this. That means starting with your positioning - who you are, who you serve, and what makes you different - and building a site structure and copy strategy that communicates that story clearly to the right audience. Combined with search engine optimization, the result is a site that doesn't just look good: it gets found, and it converts.

What's Included

Strategy, copy, and search visibility - built together.

Website Strategy & Positioning: A clear articulation of your value proposition, your audience, and the story your site needs to tell. This is the foundation everything else is built on.

Copywriting: Page-by-page website copy written in your voice - compelling, clear, and built around how your audience actually thinks and searches. Not generic nonprofit-speak. Not AI-generated filler. Writing that sounds like a human being who cares.

I approach SEO as a content problem, not a technical one - because sustainable search rankings come from creating content people actually want to read.

Content Strategy: A plan for the ongoing content that keeps your site fresh, relevant, and authoritative in your area. Blog posts, resources, thought leadership - built around what your audience is searching for.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Why is SEO important for nonprofits?

SEO helps the people already looking for what you do find you in search results. For nonprofits, that means prospective donors, volunteers, funders, and people seeking your services. Organizations that rank well get a steady stream of warm, qualified traffic without ongoing advertising spend. Strong search visibility is one of the highest-return investments a small nonprofit can make in its digital presence.

What does nonprofit website copywriting involve?

Nonprofit website copywriting translates your mission into language that resonates with your audience - donors, volunteers, funders, and beneficiaries. Good website copy is clear about what you do and who you serve, tells stories rather than just stating facts, and gives visitors a compelling reason to get involved. It's also written with search intent in mind, using the language your audience actually searches.

What is on-page SEO for a nonprofit website?

On-page SEO means optimizing individual pages to rank well for relevant queries. It includes keyword research, compelling title tags and meta descriptions, clear heading structure, fast load times, mobile responsiveness, and content that comprehensively answers the questions your audience is asking. It's fundamentally a content problem - which is why I approach SEO as a writer first.

How long does it take to see results from nonprofit SEO?

Results typically take three to six months to become measurable, and six to twelve months to reach full impact. The timeline depends on your current domain authority, keyword competitiveness, and how consistently you produce new content. Organizations that invest in ongoing content creation see compounding returns over time as their site builds topical authority.
